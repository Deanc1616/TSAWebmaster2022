Events



5k Runs



For those who are eager run a lot, 12.5 laps is a 5k run! Also, if participating in one of our various road 5ks, then you will be able to experience different surfaces on an out and back, or circle-style course.







We Offer:





















Riverton 4th of July 5k

This historic race has been going on consistently for many years now in the community, and will continue to for many more. A holiday tradition, as runners run from Sacred Heart Church around all the way to Riverton Park, while getting to see most of the town, it's a perfect way to celebrate America's Independence as well as have some fun with fitness!









Moorestown Turkey Trot 5k

Another holiday race, with many similarities as well as differences. This race is exclusively around Moorestown High School, which is mostly on soft ground rather than hard concrete. Additionally, the cold weather during November will make this race a bit tougher, so be prepared! When finishing the race, you go onto Moorestown's track for almost a lap of an all out sprint, to finish off and hopefully enjoy food with family and friends afterward!

























St. Charles Rock n' Roll Back to School 5k

This race has been retired for a few years, but no longer! A Labor Day classic for many, this race would be held at St. Charles Borromeo School in Cinnaminson, New Jersey, and be ran through the neighborhoods. In fact, it was the only out and back course on this list, and similar to the 4th of July 5k, is exclusively on hard concrete. However, it is a fun, family-friendly event to bring back the joy of running to people of all ages!





1 Mile



For the 1 mile run, you start slightly behind the 400m starting point, as a mile is 1609 meters. You need to run 4 laps around the track, which will then get you one of the longest standing middle/long distance events out there!

800 Meter



Two laps around the track, the half mile or 800 meter race is a sort of middle distance race that is tough to pace. Some say run it like a 400 and try to hold on, while others say go out slower and close fast. Either way, it is a fun race to try to run quickly that can unite sprinters and distance runners alike.

100 Meter Dash*



For those who want to race agasint the clock in a fast race, sprinting is for you! This is the shortest event in outdoor track and field, and is accesible to any athlete of any age!





200 Meter Dash



Similar to the 100m dash, 200 meters covers a greater distance. Still meant for short distance sprinters, but tests a longer, more enduring sprint where you get up to and then maintain a top end speed.


400 Meter Dash*



Double the distance of the 200m sprints, the 400m sprints cover the largest sprint, starting to get into the territory of middle distance running. It is one full lap around the track, so it's easy to track, and can again be done by almost anyone!











*Means that the event has the option to do hurdles with it, availible for the 100 Meter, 110 Meter, and 400 meter Races
